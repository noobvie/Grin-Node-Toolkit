What is Grin?

Grin is a privacy-preserving cryptocurrency implementing the MimbleWimble protocol. It has no addresses, no visible amounts, and uses Confidential Transactions so only sender and receiver can see what was transferred.

Key Properties

PrivacyCT + CoinJoin; no amounts on-chain
No AddressesTransactions via interactive Slatepack
Emission1 GRIN/sec forever — no halving
PoW AlgorithmCuckatoo32+ (ASIC-friendly, GPU-capable)
Block Time60 seconds
Block Reward60 GRIN (1 GRIN/sec × 60s)
LaunchJanuary 15, 2019 (no premine, no ICO)
Founder RewardNone
Max SupplyNone (infinite linear emission)

Why Grin?

New to crypto? Here are the things that make Grin genuinely different — explained simply.

⏱
Time-backed money — a genuinely new idea
Every currency in history has been backed by something: gold by a scarce metal, dollars by a government promise, Bitcoin by a programmed scarcity with halving steps. Grin is backed by something never tried before: time itself. One minute of real-world clock time creates exactly 60 GRIN — always, without exception, without anyone's permission. You cannot fake time, speed it up, or print more of it. This makes Grin the first monetary policy that is truly neutral — not controlled by any person, company, or committee. Enforced by physics, verified by mathematics, distributed to whoever mines each block. A currency where the supply is as objective and unstoppable as the passage of time.
⚖
Fair for everyone, forever
No halvings, no early-investor advantage. 60 GRIN per minute — the same rule whether you discover it today or in 20 years. Everyone plays by identical terms.
👁
Private by design, not by option
Amounts and parties are hidden in the cryptographic math of every transaction. Nobody can scan the blockchain to see your balance or trace who paid whom.
🏦
No custodian risk
Tokenised gold needs a custodian who can be frozen or shut down. Grin's supply is verified by open-source math on your own computer. No company in the middle, ever.
🖨
Immune to money printing
Stablecoins tied to USD silently lose value when governments expand the money supply. Grin's emission is hard-coded in protocol — no committee can ever change it.
₿
Solves Bitcoin's security cliff
Bitcoin stops minting after 21M coins, leaving miners to survive on fees alone. Grin always pays miners a fixed reward — network security doesn't have an expiry date.
🪶
A blockchain that stays small
Grin automatically removes spent data from the chain. After 7+ years live, the full history is ~5 GB pruned. Fast to sync, cheap to run a node at home.
🌍
Designed for generations
Inflation falls smoothly every year with no cliffs or shocks. Built to still be fair and functional for people who discover it decades from now.
🔍
Simple enough to verify yourself
No hidden allocations, no founder rewards, no complex tokenomics. The codebase is small enough for any engineer to read and audit end-to-end.
⟳
Time can't be forked
Anyone can clone Grin's code, but not its history. Years of accumulated proof-of-work, protocol maturity, and community trust are baked into the chain. You can't fork time.
